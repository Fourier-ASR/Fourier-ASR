Fourier-ASR:
Benchmarking Coordinate-MLPs in Audio Signal Representations
and Learning Continuous Signals via Fourier Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks

Anonymous

Although implicit neural representations based on Coordinate-MLPs have achieved significant success in representing raw data for radiance fields, 3D shapes, and images, there remains a lack of exploration in the domain of audio signal representation. To fill this research gap, we propose a comprehensive benchmark of Coordinate-MLPs for audio signal representation, focusing on their nonlinear mappings, activation functions and positional encoding. Our benchmark reveals that while positional encoding in Coordinate-MLPs enhances their ability to represent high-frequency information, it requires intricate hyperparameter tuning. Furthermore, the insufficient nonlinear representation capability of activation functions in capturing local periodic signals, coupled with their dependence on well-initialized schemes, results in poor robustness and generalization of Coordinate-MLPs. To overcome these challenges, we introduce a novel implicit audio representation framework, Fourier-ASR, based on Fourier series theorem and Kolmogorov-Arnold representation theorem. Fourier-ASR replaces MLP networks with Fourier basis function-based Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (Fourier-KAN), eliminating the need for additional positional encoding and activation functions. Additionally, to expedite convergence of Fourier-KAN, we introduce a frequency-adaptive learning strategy (FaLS) to prevent overfitting of low-frequency signals and effectively capture high-frequency components and local periodicity in audio signals. Experimental results on datasets comprising natural speech and music demonstrate: (1) effective enhancement of audio representation quality through well-designed positional encoding and activation functions in Coordinate-MLPs, and (2) robust and high-fidelity audio representations achieved by Fourier-ASR with minimal parameter adjustments. Lastly, given the foundational nature of audio representation, our research holds potential applications in downstream tasks such as audio compression, denoising, synthesis, and generation.

Baselines

The following results compare SIREN to a variety of network architectures. TanH, ReLU, Softplus etc. means an MLP of equal size with the respective nonlinearity. We also compare to the recently proposed positional encoding, combined with a ReLU nonlinearity, noted as ReLU P.E. SIREN outperforms all baselines by a significant margin, converges significantly faster, and is the only architecture that accurately represents the gradients of the signal, enabling its use to solve boundary value problems.

Representing Audio

A Siren with a single, time-coordinate input and scalar output may parameterize audio signals. Siren is the only network architecture that succeeds in reproducing the audio signal, both for music and human voice.
